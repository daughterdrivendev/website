## Winter is here. 

Snow is piling up across Europe and other parts of the Northern Hemisphere. Slippery roads, canceled flights and trains. In the ideal movie version of parenthood at this time of year, parents build snowmen and go sledding down the perfect hill, scarves flapping in the wind.

### Reality, however, looks a bit different. 

The most anticipated information for us is posted on the display board at the kindergarten entrance. During this season, kids do not collect and trade Pokémon cards. They collect and trade illnesses.

A short summary from the board in our youngest child’s group during the last two weeks before Christmas: gastrointestinal infections (multiple times), hand, foot, and mouth disease (multiple times), scarlet fever (once), COVID (a lot).

And of course, the daily horror continues into the new year. Questions start popping up in our heads. How do you politely, without sounding like a terrible person, tell your child that maybe they should not hug and wrestle with Paul, whose entire T-shirt is covered in nasal secretions?

### Other questions follow quickly. 

How do you deal with the fallout from all of this? How do you throw away carefully planned schedules and time-boxed calendars? How do you tell your team that a deadline that once seemed very achievable might now be a stretch? Because yes, this small human needs us right now.

Every winter, we hear dozens of stories from parents. Some are painfully funny. Others create an instant sense of solidarity. And occasionally, you hear a genuinely helpful idea about how to cope with all of this.

So what is your story? How do you cope?
